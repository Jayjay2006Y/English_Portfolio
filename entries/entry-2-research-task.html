Diminutives in Australian English

A feature of Australian English: shortening words to sound casual and friendly.

A unique feature of Australian English is the frequent use of diminutives. Australians like to shorten words and add endings such as -ie/-y or -o. This creates a casual, friendly tone in everyday conversation.

Examples

  • tradie = tradesperson
  • arvo = afternoon
  • ambo = ambulance driver
  • servo = service station (gas station)
  • footy = football

This pattern shows the informal and playful nature of Australian English.
